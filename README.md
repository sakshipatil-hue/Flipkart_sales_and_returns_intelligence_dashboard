###  Flipkart Sales & Returns Intelligence Dashboard

End-to-end data analytics project analyzing 1,00,000+ orders across 2 years
using Python, MySQL, and Power BI — uncovering pricing patterns, festive season impact, return behavior, and seller performance across Indian e-commerce.


## 📌 Project Overview
This project simulates the work of a data analyst at an Indian e-commerce company. Starting from a real Flipkart product catalog ,
I built a complete multi-table transactional database, performed SQL-based analytics,
and delivered a one-page interactive Power BI dashboard answering 8 real business questions.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack
Python        → Data cleaning, feature engineering, synthetic data generation
MySQL         → Star schema design, analytical queries, views
Power BI      → Interactive one-page dashboard, DAX measures
Pandas        → ETL pipeline, data transformation
SQLAlchemy    → Python to MySQL connection

## 📊 Key Insights
# 1. Big Billion Days generates 4x revenue per day
Big Billion Days (Oct 1–7) produces approximately 4x the revenue of a regular day. Electronics and Clothing categories see the sharpest spikes during this period.
# 2. Optimal discount band is 20–40%
Products discounted between 20–40% receive the highest average ratings. Beyond 40% discount, average ratings drop — over-discounting attracts low-intent buyers who rate poorly.
# 3. Clothing has the highest return rate
Clothing returns at ~18% driven primarily by size issues. Electronics returns are driven by defective products. High-return categories directly erode net GMV.
# 4. UPI dominates but COD customers order less
UPI accounts for 38% of all orders. Cash on Delivery customers have 22% lower average order value — suggesting COD is associated with lower-trust, price-sensitive buyers.
# 5. Festive season cohorts retain better
Customers who made their first purchase during October (Big Billion Days) show 12% higher retention rates than customers acquired in other months — festive buyers have higher intent.
# 6. Top 3 categories drive 60%+ of GMV
Electronics, Clothing, and Footwear together account for over 60% of total estimated GMV — classic Pareto concentration creating platform revenue risk.
# 7. Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru drive 35% of revenue
Three cities account for over a third of total platform revenue despite representing a small fraction of total customers — premium urban buyers have significantly higher LTV
